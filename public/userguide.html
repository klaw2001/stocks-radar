How It Works

Results Radar runs a fully automated pipeline in the background. Here is what happens from the moment a company publishes results to when you see the data on your screen.

📡

Step 1 — Stock Scan detects new results

Every 10 minutes, the system checks the Stock Scan screener for new earnings events. When a company files results, a new row is automatically added to your dashboard with the date, price, and financial metrics.

💬

Step 2 — Social sentiment is checked

The platform searches for recent public chatter about the company. Based on tone, posts are classified as Good, Neutral, or Bad sentiment, giving you a market pulse at a glance.

🎙️

Step 3 — Earnings call transcript is found

Every 30 minutes, the system checks for conference call transcripts from management. When one becomes available, the row status changes from Pending to Available.

🤖

Step 4 — AI summary is generated

An AI model reads the transcript and produces a structured summary — key highlights, guidance, management tone, and financial performance — so you can understand the call in under 2 minutes.

👁️

Step 5 — You review at your pace

Log in, scan the dashboard, read the AI summary, mark items as read, and filter by sentiment or transcript status. Everything is organised so you can prioritise what matters most.

Common Use Cases

📋

Scenario A — Morning results check

Start your day by seeing what companies reported overnight

  1. Log in to Results Radar. The dashboard opens automatically on today's date.
  2. Look at the New Today card at the top — it shows how many companies filed results.
  3. Scan down the table. Companies with a green SALES YOY beat last year's revenue.
  4. Click the Transcript Pending filter tab to see which companies have not yet published their management call.
  5. For companies you've reviewed, click the circle in the STATUS column to mark them read.
⏱️

A full morning check typically takes 5–10 minutes on the dashboard before diving deeper into individual names.

🎙️

Scenario B — Reading an earnings call summary

Get the key takeaways from management in under 2 minutes

  1. Click the Transcript Available filter tab to show only companies whose call transcripts are ready.
  2. Find the company you want to review. Look for the 🤖 button in the ACTIONS column.
  3. Click 🤖 — a panel slides in from the right with the AI-generated summary.
  4. The summary is broken into sections: Financial Performance, Key Highlights, Management Guidance, and more.
  5. To read the full transcript, click the 📄 Full Transcript link at the top of the panel.
  6. Press Escape or click the ✕ button to close the panel.
📊

Scenario C — Screening by sentiment

Find companies with positive or negative market buzz quickly

  1. Click the Good Chatter filter tab to see companies with positive social sentiment.
  2. Check the CHATTER column — you'll see Good, Neutral, or Bad for each company.
  3. Companies with Bad sentiment may have disappointed the market — cross-check the financials to see why.
  4. Use the search box at the top to jump directly to a specific company by name.
  5. Combine filters — e.g., Transcript Available + Good Chatter — by clicking one tab, searching, and reviewing results.
💡

Sentiment is a signal, not a verdict. Always pair it with the actual financial numbers before drawing conclusions.

Quick Glossary

YOY (Year on Year)
Compares this period's number to the same period last year. A +20% YOY sales figure means the company grew revenue by 20% versus a year ago.
PAT (Profit After Tax)
The company's net profit — what's left after all expenses and taxes are paid. Also called "net income".
EBITDA
Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation. A measure of operating profitability that strips out financing and accounting effects.
EPS (Earnings Per Share)
Net profit divided by the number of shares. Higher EPS generally means more value for shareholders.
P/E (Price-to-Earnings Ratio)
Stock price divided by EPS. It tells you how much the market pays for each rupee of profit. A high P/E can mean high growth expectations.
Market Cap (Cr)
Total market value of all shares, expressed in Indian Rupee crores (1 crore = 10 million).
Transcript
A written record of everything said during the company's earnings conference call — management's speech and analyst Q&A.
Chatter Sentiment
AI assessment of public posts and discussions about the company's results. Classified as Good, Neutral, or Bad.
